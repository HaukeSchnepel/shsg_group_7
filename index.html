​ Your Analysis Results

​ Slope Development - between Shoulders
​
Lateral Pelvic Tilt Recommendations:
  • Large deviations in the shoulder slope imply that there is an asymmetric positioning of the pelvis or in other words: One waist height is higher or lower than the other side which is reflected in the positioning of your shoulders. This can lead to being more prone to injury, reduced mobility and can negatively affect our overall physical performance.
  • To prevent this, we recommend the following:
    • Try to not lean to one side
    • If you are already experiencing pain, you may want to see a chiropractor


​​ Distance Development - Closeness to Computer
​
Forward Head Posture Recommendations:
  • When the distances between both shoulders are very high, this implies that you have moved very close to the camera. This means that when you sit at your desk with a buckled back. This can lead to long-term neck and back issues.
  • To prevent this, we recommend the following:
    • Chin tuck
    • Chest stretches
    • Set up workspace ergonomically
    • Sleep on a cervical pillow
  • If you want to know more about it we recommend clicking on the following link: https://www.physio-pedia.com/Forward_Head_Posture


​ Overall
Generally, for a good sitting position we recommend to:
  • Sit up with your back straight and your shoulders back. Your buttocks should touch the back of your chair.
  • Bend your knees at a right angle. Keep your knees even with or slightly higher than your hips. Do not cross your legs.
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor.
  • Try to avoid sitting in the same position for more than 30 minutes.
    • Check out stretching and posture exercises to do while sitting at your desk.
    • Also see back strengthening courses at the HSG university gym: https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/hsgservices/sport
